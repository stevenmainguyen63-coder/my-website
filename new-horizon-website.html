The Journey

A life relearned

From war and displacement, to fatherhood, to a moment that changed how I understood everything.

I was born as Steven Mai Nguyen, also known as Lai Mai — a child of two cultures, half Chinese, half Vietnamese — raised during a time when the world around me was filled with fear and uncertainty. My earliest memories were not of toys or playgrounds, but of air-raid sirens and families running for safety.

As a child I was fascinated by stories of the unseen world. An elderly neighbour told ghost stories at night, and although they frightened me, they also awakened a deep curiosity: where do we come from, and where do we go? But life pushed those questions aside. I told myself life was only one — you are born once, you live once, you die once.

Destiny had other plans. My family fled across Southeast Asia searching for a place to survive. Ten years passed before we reached stable ground — by then, my childhood was gone. I became a young man, a husband, and a father of three, working tirelessly to give my children the life I never had, while supporting my aging parents.

"We are sorry for your hardships, but we have never harmed your children. Life is a learning journey. Without challenges, how would you grow?"

Twenty-seven years ago, something happened that reconnected me with the spiritual world I had abandoned as a child. I cried — not from fear, but from the weight of everything I had lived through. In that moment I asked why life had to be so hard, and the answer I felt was gentle, not angry. I woke up. I understood. From that day, I began learning again — not from books, but from life, experience, and the quiet voice inside me.

Today I stand here not because my journey is finished, but because I am completing my final lessons. I am still a student of life, and every person I meet becomes either my teacher or my classmate. I created New Horizon of Human Spirits because I believe we can build something better — not just for ourselves, but for the generations after us.
